What is Apple CarPlay and Android Auto

Apple CarPlay was released in 2014 and Android Auto in 2015. Both systems display basic smartphone functions on the car’s multimedia screen.

This is exactly what the driver needs while driving: handsfree voice calls, music player, navigation maps and incoming messages. In addition, having a smart system is significantly different from connecting an analog phone to a car – in fact, you fully control your smartphone using the buttons on the steering wheel, without being distracted from the road.

A multimedia system with Apple CarPlay or Android Auto is an excellent opportunity to quickly synchronise your smartphone with the car. This way you can either leave your phone in the holder or put it away so you don’t have to be distracted from the road.

  • Apple CarPlay. Connecting an iOS device via Wi-Fi without a cable;
  • Android Auto. Using applications on Android smartphones without a cable;
  • Mirror Link. Duplicating your smartphone screen to your car screen;
  • Camera. Possibility of connecting the front and rear cameras of the car;
  • Bluetooth. Using standard bluetooth connection for music and calls;
  • USB Flash. Playing movies and music from a USB drive.

How Android Auto differs from Apple CarPlay

In general, this system, like Apple CarPlay, has a standard set of functions.

Among them:

  • hands free, linked to the car’s multimedia system;
  • the ability to receive calls and SMS using voice commands, and independently call your contacts;
  • synchronization with popular instant messengers (Telegram, Viber, WhatsApp), allowing you to dictate and listen to SMS;
  • support for maps, even in offline mode (including voice navigators);
  • the ability to display only useful applications on the taskbar;
  • listen to your favourite music, podcasts, audio books, plays, set up a radio.
